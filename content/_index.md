I am Chunyu Liu, a third-year PhD student in Mental Health, psychiatric genomics track, at Johns Hopkins University, working with [Brion Maher](https://publichealth.jhu.edu/faculty/2471/brion-maher) and [Adam P. Spira](https://publichealth.jhu.edu/faculty/2102/adam-p-spira), and a concurrent master's student in biostatistics, working with [Nilanjan Chatterjee](https://publichealth.jhu.edu/faculty/1826/nilanjan-chatterjee) and [Leonardo Collado Torres](https://publichealth.jhu.edu/faculty/4647/leonardo-collado-torres).

My work develops quantitative methods to study dementia, neurodegeneration, immune function, and biological aging using multi-omics and longitudinal data.

## Research Focus

I integrate genomic, epigenetic, proteomic, immune, and longitudinal phenotype data to identify mechanisms and risk signatures for dementia and brain aging, with sleep and circadian disruption as key exposures.
