I am a PhD student in Mental Health at Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health with training in psychiatric genomics, biostatistics, and epidemiology.

## Background

Before Hopkins, I completed an M.S. in Epidemiology and Biostatistics at Peking University Health Science Center, working with [Jun Lv](https://sph.pku.edu.cn/info/1317/4044.htm), and dual bachelor's degrees in Preventive Medicine and Psychological/Cognitive Science at Peking University. During that period, I worked with China Kadoorie Biobank data on GWAS, polygenic risk scoring, and Mendelian randomization, with a broad interest in how behavioral exposures shape long-term chronic disease risk in older adults. I also worked with [L. H. Lumey](https://www.publichealth.columbia.edu/profile/l-h-lumey-md) and [Chi Hua Li](https://sklmqcm.um.edu.mo/chi-hua-li/) on natural experiments, causal inference, and method development for demographic and epidemiologic studies.

## Research Direction

My research focuses on dementia, neurodegeneration, immune function, and biological aging through the integration of genomic, epigenetic, proteomic, and longitudinal phenotype data.

Current work spans sleep and biological aging, immune-functional genomics, and multi-omics approaches to dementia and brain aging. Detailed project descriptions are available on the [Projects](/projects/) page.
